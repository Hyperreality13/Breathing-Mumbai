Why 2025–2026 Was Worse
Westerly wind disturbances caused easterly winds to slow significantly during winter. This triggered the first-ever GRAP Stage 4 in Mumbai (Nov 28, 2025). Areas like Mazgaon, Malad, Andheri East and Borivali East saw AQI exceed 300 for multiple consecutive days with no nighttime recovery — a pattern not seen since SAFAR began tracking 6 years ago.

Seasonal Impact
Winter (Nov–Feb): AQI 150–300+ due to reduced wind and temperature inversions.

Monsoon (Jun–Sep): AQI drops to 50–90 as rain and strong winds clean the air.

Research Insights
Key findings from the data investigation
Core Finding
Mumbai's air quality is not just determined by how much pollution is produced — it's determined by how well the city can disperse it.
AQI is often treated as a single number. But in Mumbai, the same emission levels on a day with strong sea breeze versus a calm winter morning produce radically different outcomes. The system — wind, density, season, construction — matters as much as the source.

Research Questions — Answered
Why more frequent poor AQI despite coastal location?
Post-COVID construction boom + growing vehicle fleet + weakening winter winds. 2026 AQI is 64.2% worse than the 2020–2025 average.
How does wind affect AQI?
Wind is the primary dispersal mechanism. The same emissions produce AQI 80 with strong wind or 300+ with no wind. Sea breeze can clear smog within hours.
Why does a coastal city still suffer?
Temperature inversions + seasonal weakening of sea breeze + urban density blocking airflow in eastern suburbs all compound the problem in winter.
About This Project
Why Mumbai Can't Breathe — Data Visualization, 2026
Description
"Why Mumbai Can't Breathe" is an interactive data visualization investigating the environmental and urban systems behind Mumbai's worsening air quality. Rather than presenting AQI as a static number, the project transforms it into a live, reactive experience — allowing users to manipulate wind, traffic, construction, and seasonal factors and observe in real time how each variable drives pollution across the city.
Target Audience
Urban individuals aged 18–25 in Mumbai who regularly encounter AQI data but lack deeper understanding of what causes it to change. Students, young professionals, and daily commuters.
Core Insight
AQI is not just about how much pollution is generated — it is determined equally by how effectively the city disperses it. Wind, urban density, and seasonal patterns are as important as emission sources.
How to Use the Dashboard
① Use Wind Speed buttons to see sea breeze dispersion in action
② Increase Traffic intensity to add more emission particles
③ Toggle Construction / Waste Burning / Winter to layer factors
④ Watch the canvas — particles accumulate or clear in real time
⑤ The AQI number smoothly transitions to show combined effect
